# Top 5 Donor Search Products: Analysis of User Sentiment

## Overview

This document provides a structured analysis of the top 5 donor prospect research and wealth screening products used by nonprofits. For each product, we include what users love, what users hate, key features, pricing context, and ideal use cases.

---

## 1. iWave (by Kindsight)

### Description
iWave is a comprehensive donor research and wealth screening platform that aggregates data from over 44+ vetted sources into unified 360Â° donor profiles. It is consistently ranked #1 on G2 for donor research software for 17 consecutive quarters.

### What Users Love
- **Ease of use**: Intuitive interface with well-marked tabs and sections for screenings, profile creation, and news access
- **Comprehensive data consolidation**: Collects many different data points onto one screen, saving significant research time
- **Clear presentation**: Information retrieved is easy to read and understand with minimal training required
- **Data categories**: Good value for cost with excellent search functionality
- **Customizable outputs**: Ability to dive deep into sources and access underlying data
- **Strong customer support**: Responsive company that listens to customer feedback
- **Regular feature updates**: New features added yearly without cost increases
- **Company and foundation screening**: Only tool that can comprehensively screen companies and foundations

### What Users Hate
- **Missing data for some donors**: Can retrieve minimal information if specific details aren't available
- **Limited email address verification**: Users wish for better email address confirmation capabilities
- **Canadian donor limitations**: Some features like filtering by indicators not available for Canadian donors
- **New version regressions**: Less flexible search parameters sometimes miss real estate data and SEC events
- **Interface can be slow**: Some users report occasional slowness and confusing tabs
- **Credit-based system**: Uses credits for searches which some find limiting
- **Matching confidence**: Room for improvement in confidence scoring for data matching
- **No spouse search**: Cannot search for spouse along with original name
- **No mobile app**: Lacks mobile application

### Pricing Context
- Starts at approximately $3,545/year for Starter plan
- Premium and Professional plans available with custom pricing
- Uses credit-based system (~1,000 credits included)
- Approximately $4,900/year with 1,000 credits

### Best For
- Organizations prioritizing high-quality, actionable data over quick list-building
- Nonprofits seeking precise donor intelligence for major gifts and legacy contributions
- Teams wanting to screen companies and foundations in addition to individuals
- Users who value accuracy and customization over speed

## 2. DonorSearch

### Description
DonorSearch provides the most comprehensive prospect research database on the market with over one billion data points, updated weekly from dozens of trusted sources. It boasts an accuracy rate above 90% and integrates with more than 40 nonprofit CRMs.

### What Users Love
- **User-friendly interface**: Easy to learn and navigate with little training required
- **Comprehensive and accurate data**: High-quality, useful information that impresses nonprofit clients
- **Excellent customer service**: Phenomenal support with designated team members who respond within 24 hours
- **Regular data updates**: Commitment to updating data and platform regularly
- **DS scores and rankings**: Helpful for transforming data into actionable lists
- **Manual prospect verification**: Top prospects manually verified by research team
- **Extensive training resources**: Monthly webinars, online trainings, and excellent documentation
- **Strong CRM integrations**: Works well with 40+ existing tools
- **Focus on philanthropy**: 100% focused on nonprofit fundraising (not luxury markets)

### What Users Hate
- **Clunky and outdated UI**: Some find the user interface confusing and difficult compared to competitors
- **Overwhelming information**: Abundance of data can be challenging to navigate effectively
- **Duplicate records**: After integrated searches, deduping results can be messy
- **Manual RENXT integration**: Time-consuming manual process between DonorSearch and RENXT
- **Pricing concerns**: May be too expensive for smaller organizations
- **Requires specific details**: Challenging to find donors when only minor information is available
- **Individual record loading**: Must load screening results for each record individually in larger operations

### Pricing Context
- Approximately $4,000/year
- Pricing based on number of "pulls" or contacts screened
- Custom pricing based on database size and features needed

### Best For
- Organizations wanting verified, accurate philanthropic data
- Nonprofits who value human-verified research over purely algorithmic approaches
- Healthcare, education, arts/culture organizations
- Teams wanting comprehensive training and support resources

## 3. WealthEngine

### Description
WealthEngine is a predictive marketing analytics and wealth intelligence platform with half a trillion data points. It uses machine learning to develop and pre-score 250 million profiles with over 5 million refreshes weekly.

### What Users Love
- **Level of data detail**: Comprehensive wealth intelligence with deep insights
- **RealQuest Property Search**: Particularly useful for researching prospects with significant real estate
- **Donor pyramid modeler**: Automates creation of accurate donor segments for long-term fundraising strategies
- **Training resources**: Heap of training materials and support to help nonprofits get started
- **CRM integrations**: Strong integrations with Salesforce and other platforms
- **Wealth scores and ratings**: Helpful for identifying, segmenting, and prioritizing donors by net worth, cash on hand, propensity to give
- **Political donation tracking**: Can analyze political contributions to develop donor profiles
- **Versatility**: Used successfully by schools, healthcare, nonprofits, and commercial organizations

### What Users Hate
- **High cost**: Price is prohibitive for smaller nonprofits ($5,500+/year to start)
- **No effective mobile app**: Lack of mobile application is a concern for some users
- **Not nonprofit-specific**: Designed for multiple industries so may not cater specifically to nonprofit needs
- **Implementation costs**: Can range from $2,000 to $20,000 depending on complexity
- **Profile not actively managed**: G2 profile hasn't been claimed/updated in over a year
- **Learning curve**: Some features can be complex to master

### Pricing Context
- Approximately $5,500/year for 1-year subscription (no records screened)
- $15,000/year for 10 users
- $50,000/year for 100 users
- Custom pricing for enterprise
- Implementation costs: $2,000-$20,000

### Best For
- Mid-to-large nonprofits with substantial budgets
- Organizations needing both commercial and nonprofit wealth intelligence
- Schools for wealth screening, admissions, and fundraising
- Teams wanting automated donor pyramid modeling
- Organizations prioritizing real estate wealth indicators

## 4. Blackbaud ResearchPoint

### Description
ResearchPoint is Blackbaud's prospect research solution that integrates directly with Raiser's Edge NXT. It combines wealth screening with predictive analytics and delivers donor and prospect data in a single platform.

### What Users Love
- **Raiser's Edge integration**: Seamless syncing with Blackbaud's ecosystem
- **Researcher customization**: Ability to add or edit verified information, do own calculations
- **Complete prospect picture**: Allows viewing a prospect as a whole before meetings
- **Wealth and giving history screening**: Comprehensive view of nonprofit/giving involvement
- **Target Analytics**: Helpful for prioritizing outreach and analyzing organizational wellbeing
- **Predictive modeling**: AI-powered insights for major gift candidates
- **Property and business assets**: Can find and add records that didn't appear in initial screening
- **Profile reporting**: Highlights top major gift candidates effectively

### What Users Hate
- **Annual data sync only**: Data updated on Raiser's Edge only once a year
- **Separate application required**: Must open another application to verify current data
- **Confusing software elements**: Some parts of Target Analytics are confusing
- **Incomplete reports**: Doesn't always provide all parts of reports needed
- **Search features need work**: Search functionality could be improved
- **Granularity changes**: Recent changes to capacity ranges (e.g., $1-$10,000 lowest range) less useful for smaller organizations
- **Training investment required**: Need to complete training to understand all features and get ROI

### Pricing Context
- Approximately $5,000/year
- Often bundled with Blackbaud CRM products
- Custom pricing based on organization size

### Best For
- Organizations already using Blackbaud Raiser's Edge NXT
- Nonprofits wanting tight CRM-research integration
- Large institutions (museums, higher education, healthcare)
- Teams with dedicated prospect research staff willing to invest in training

## 5. Windfall

### Description
Windfall is an AI-powered wealth intelligence platform focused on providing accurate, up-to-date net worth data. It rebuilds datasets weekly and focuses on individuals with net worths greater than one million dollars.

### What Users Love
- **Data accuracy**: Weekly data updates provide most up-to-date information
- **Simplicity**: Easy to use and understand
- **Unlimited screening**: Subscriptions include unlimited screening (no credits/pulls)
- **AI-powered insights**: Predictive and generative AI features streamline scores and recommendations
- **Hidden gem discovery**: Users report finding donors they didn't know existed
- **Life event triggers**: Tracks recent moves, career changes, multi-property ownership for timely outreach
- **Modern integrations**: Bi-directional integrations with popular CRMs like Bloomerang, Neon CRM
- **Career intelligence**: 25+ career and firmographic attributes for 360-degree constituent view
- **ROI results**: Documented cases of 4X revenue increases and 20% increase in major gift prospects

### What Users Hate
- **Algorithmic accuracy concerns**: AI might make mistakes; not as many customer reviews verifying accuracy yet
- **No manual verification**: Unlike DonorSearch, doesn't have human verification of top prospects
- **Limited CRM integrations**: Missing some widely used options (e.g., Blackbaud Raiser's Edge NXT)
- **Focus on high-net-worth only**: Originally focused on millionaires, though expanding to full donor pyramid
- **Less established**: Newer player compared to iWave and DonorSearch
- **Customer service complaints**: At least one public review expressing serious dissatisfaction
- **Less comprehensive philanthropic data**: Not as strong on giving history to other organizations

### Pricing Context
- Contact for pricing (not publicly shared)
- Includes unlimited screening in subscription
- No per-record or credit fees

### Best For
- Organizations focused on major gifts and planned giving
- Nonprofits wanting weekly-updated wealth data
- Teams using Bloomerang, Neon CRM, or other integrated platforms
- Organizations prioritizing net worth accuracy over philanthropic history
- Data-driven teams wanting AI-powered recommendations

## Key Takeaways for Product Selection

1. **Best overall for accuracy and support**: DonorSearch - with 90%+ accuracy and human verification
2. **Best for Blackbaud users**: ResearchPoint - seamless integration with Raiser's Edge NXT
3. **Best for ease of use and comprehensive data**: iWave - most recommended by industry professionals
4. **Best for wealth intelligence**: WealthEngine - deepest wealth data and modeling tools
5. **Best for modern AI-driven insights**: Windfall - weekly updates and predictive AI features
